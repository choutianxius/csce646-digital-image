Team Project Proposal: Black Hole

Theron Smith, Marina Trevino, Tianxiu (Tyson) Zhou

Idea and Expected Results

We are recreating the following image in a dynamic way using lighting and movement.
We will separate the background spiral from the foreground spheres to create the illusion that the spheres are going into the center of the spiral and that the background is rotating. We will create 3D spheres to represent the orbs from the original image to enable lighting changes on the orbs. We will create an array of the orbs with a slider that changes the number of orbs on screen, the speed of the spiral rotation, and the lighting on the orbs.

Proposed Implementation Process

  • Swirling orbs: The spiral orbits of the orbs can be achieved by using a combination of cosine ans sine functions, with varying orbit radius based on time. The moving speed of the orbs can be adjusted by applying a scaling function on time.
  • Shrinking orbs: As the orbs approach the center, their radius should decrease. This can be achieved by calculating the orb radius proportional to their orbit radius.
  • Multiple orbs: We may fine-tune the entrance locations of the orbs and their initial/ending sizes, so that the final shader looks continuous. We may even allow the users to specify how many orbs should be shown in the shader.
  • Orb image: We can separate the orbs from the original preliminary image and use them as the texture of the orbs in the shader.
  • Swirling background: We should be able to calculate the swirling background from math, and apply texture to make it look like the background in our preliminary image.
  • Lighting: We can combine the height images of the orbs with the calculated height of the background, and calculate the light accordingly. We may combine two light sources, one at the center at the swirl, and the other dynamically set by the user's mouse.
